{{milestone:{label:tr,id:UT22084-090-033-translation}}}The Dhāraṇī-Mantra to Have the Entire Noble “Sūtra of Descent into Laṅkā” Read
{{milestone:{label:1.1,id:UT22084-090-033-52}}}{{page:{number:1,id:UT22084-090-033-121,folio:F.204.b}}} Homage to the noble Descent into Laṅkā.
{{milestone:{label:1.2,id:UT22084-090-033-55}}}“Mahāmati, I shall speak these mantra-words: 
{{milestone:{label:1.3,id:UT22084-090-033-56}}}tadyathā—padme padmadeve{{note:{index:5,id:UT22084-090-033-57}}} hine hine hine culu culu culu hule hule hule yule yule yule pale pale pale muñce chinde{{note:{index:6,id:UT22084-090-033-58}}} bhinde bhañje{{note:{index:7,id:UT22084-090-033-59}}} marde pramarde{{note:{index:8,id:UT22084-090-033-60}}} dinakare svāhā!{{note:{index:9,id:UT22084-090-033-61}}}
{{milestone:{label:1.4,id:UT22084-090-033-62}}}“Mahāmati! If any son of good family or daughter of good family upholds{{note:{index:10,id:UT22084-090-033-63}}}{{note:{index:11,id:UT22084-090-033-120}}} these mantra-words, retains them, recites them, and masters them, then no one will be able to find a weak point in them, be that a god or a goddess, a nāga or a nāgī, a yakṣa or a yakṣī, an asura or an asurī, a garuḍa or a garuḍī, a kinnara or a kinnarī, a mahoraga or a mahoragī, a gandharva or a gandharvī, a bhūta or a bhūtī, a kumbhāṇḍa or a kumbhāṇḍī, a piśāca or a piśācī, an ostārako or an ostārakī, an apasmāra or an apasmārī, a rākṣasa or a rākṣasī, a ḍāka or a ḍākī, an ojohāra or an ojohārī, a kaṭapūtana or a kaṭapūtanī, or a human man or a woman.{{note:{index:12,id:UT22084-090-033-64}}} One who recites these mantra-words will have recited the entire Sūtra of Descent into Laṅkā. The Blessed One taught these mantra-words so that rākṣasas and others{{note:{index:13,id:UT22084-090-033-65}}} will be kept at bay.”
